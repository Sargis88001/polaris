What your child will learn

Everything school will assume they know.

Academic skills matter, but so does everything else — the ability to focus, ask for help, and manage a long morning alongside other children.

🔤

Phonics & reading readiness

Letter sounds, blending, and the patience to sit with a book. Children leave able to decode simple words and understand that reading is a skill, not a mystery.

🔢

Number sense & early math

Counting, comparing, basic addition and subtraction in hands-on, concrete contexts. Mathematical confidence built before it becomes a school subject.

🧘

Self-regulation & focus

Naming feelings, sitting with a task, asking for help calmly. The internal skills that determine how much children can absorb on their first school day.

🏫

Classroom skills & independence

Listening to instructions, taking turns, unpacking a bag, following a timetable. The practical competencies every teacher hopes for and nobody formally teaches.

FAQ

Common questions

Which schools do your graduates attend?

Many different schools — public, private, and international. We prepare children for all of them. We do not target one school or one curriculum, because readiness is readiness.

What if my child joins but struggles?

We notice quickly and talk to you within the week. We adjust the approach, never the expectation of success. Regular short check-ins with parents are built into the year, not offered only when something goes wrong.

Is four mornings a week too much for a five-year-old?

The program is specifically designed to feel manageable. Sessions have a predictable rhythm, rest built in, and no artificial pressure. Most children settle in within two weeks and ask to come on their off days.

Do you give homework?

Very occasionally, short, and always optional. We might suggest a game to play at home or a book to look at together — nothing that requires a desk and a timer. The real work happens in the room.
