What your child will learn

A full childhood, in four quiet strands.

Each session weaves together motor, language, mathematical, and social development — so children grow on every axis, not just the measurable ones.

✂️

Fine & gross motor skills

Threading, cutting, climbing, balancing — physical competence built through play, without drills or pressure. Children's bodies become tools they trust.

💬

Language & communication

Stories, songs, circle conversation, and the patience to listen. Children find their voice and learn that it matters to the people around them.

🔢

Pre-math foundations

Counting, sorting, comparing, and patterning through hands-on materials. Mathematical thinking emerges naturally, before numbers feel like a subject.

🤝

Social & emotional skills

Listening, taking turns, naming feelings, repairing a small disagreement. The skills of being with others that school will later assume every child has.

FAQ

Common questions

My child has never been in a structured setting — is that okay?

Yes. The First Steps group is specifically designed for children coming from home for the first time. Sessions are short, predictable, and very gentle. We spend the first two weeks just helping children feel safe in the room.

What does a typical morning look like?

Welcome circle, focused work (a project, puzzle, or story), active play, and a short reflection before home. The shape is the same every day — predictability is part of the method.

Can my child join mid-year?

Yes, after a short settling visit. We ask new children to come for an observation morning first so the group feels familiar before day one properly begins.

Do you provide snacks?

Parents bring a small morning snack from home. We ask for no sweets or heavily processed food, and no nut products due to allergies. Snack time is part of the social routine of the morning.
